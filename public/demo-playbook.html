The Problem: The Hidden Cost of Compliance

Every Irish limited company must file a CT1 Corporation Tax return annually. For the ~270,000 small companies in Ireland, this process typically involves:

  • Manual data entry — bank transactions transcribed into spreadsheets or legacy accounting software
  • Accountant dependency — €2,000–5,000/year for a basic compliance service
  • Delayed visibility — most business owners don't see their tax position until 6–9 months after year-end
  • Missed optimisations — complex rules (RCT credits, VAT Section 60, capital allowances) are routinely under-claimed

The result: small businesses overpay on tax, underclaim on deductions, and fly blind on profitability.

The Honest Bit: Security & Compliance Concerns

We built this to show what's possible. We're not shipping it blindly. There are four real concerns we're working through:

Concern Risk Our Position
ROS Terms of Service Revenue hasn't approved bot-driven form submissions. Automating ROS may violate their ToS. Demo shows capability only. Production requires Revenue approval or use of an official API.
Credential Handling ROS uses digital certificate (.p12) auth. Giving an AI agent access to a cert = full access to someone's tax life. We'd never store or transmit certs. Agent uses a sandboxed session on the user's own device.
Liability If the AI enters a wrong number on a tax return, who is responsible? Software? Accountant? Business owner? AI prepares, human files. Balnce always stops before submission. The liability chain stays clear.
GDPR / Data Protection Tax data is sensitive. Routing it through an AI agent adds a data processor to the chain. Balnce data stays in Supabase (EU-hosted). OpenClaw runs locally — no tax data leaves the device.

Key Takeaway

The hard part is already done. The tax computation engine is live, accurate, and applying real Irish tax law — VAT Section 59/60, CT deductibility, RCT credits, capital allowances. The form-filling is the last mile. The regulatory framework will catch up.

Demonstrated Improvement: What Balnce Gets Right

Balnce applies rules that generic AI and most accountants miss or approximate:

Rule What Generic Systems Do What Balnce Does
VAT on Hotels Apply 23% standard rate Apply 9% (second reduced rate). Flag VAT as non-recoverable under Section 60(2)(a)(i). Expense remains CT-deductible.
Meals & Entertainment Often missed as add-back Auto-flagged as non-deductible for CT. Added back to trading profit.
Diesel vs Petrol Treat all fuel the same Diesel VAT is recoverable. Petrol is not. Balnce classifies per Section 60(2)(a)(v).
RCT Credits Ignored or manual Automatically deducted from CT liability. Shows as current asset on balance sheet.
Bank Charges Sometimes treated as add-back Correctly identified as CT-deductible (VAT exempt, but not an add-back).
Director's Current A/C Manual tracking Travel allowances vs drawings auto-calculated. Net balance flows to balance sheet.

Anticipated Questions & Answers

"Who is this for?"

Irish limited companies with 1–2 directors. Primarily trades (construction, electrical, plumbing) and small tech/SaaS companies. The ones paying €3k/year for an accountant to do data entry.

"How accurate is the auto-categorisation?"

We use rule-based matching against Irish VAT and CT legislation — not a generic LLM guess. Vendor name recognition, receipt OCR, and Section 60 keyword analysis. Users can review and override. Anything uncategorised is conservatively flagged as a CT add-back until confirmed.

"Are you replacing accountants?"

We're replacing the 80% that's manual grunt work — data entry, bank reconciliation, basic compliance filing. Complex tax planning, audits, and advisory still need a human. We make the human's job easier, not redundant.

"What's the business model?"

SaaS subscription. The accountant is a €3,000/year cost that most small companies accept as unavoidable. We make it avoidable — or at least dramatically cheaper.

"How is this different from Xero/QuickBooks?"

Xero and QuickBooks are general-purpose tools that require manual categorisation and don't understand Irish tax rules. Balnce is purpose-built for Irish CT1/VAT/RCT compliance. It applies the rules automatically — Section 60 VAT, CT add-backs, two-thirds rule, capital allowances. No accountant in the loop for the basics.
