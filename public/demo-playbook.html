1 What is Balnce?

Balnce is an automated accounting system for Irish limited companies and their directors. It takes raw bank data and turns it into a complete set of accounts — P&L, Balance Sheet, VAT returns, and Corporation Tax (CT1) computation — with no manual bookkeeping.

The Problem

Small Irish LLCs (construction, trades, SaaS) spend thousands on accountants for basic compliance. Most don't look at their numbers until year-end. By then it's too late to optimise.

The Solution

Upload a bank CSV. Balnce auto-categorises every transaction using Irish tax rules (VAT Section 59/60, CT deductibility, RCT). You get a live P&L, CT1 computation, and balance sheet — instantly.

4 Security & Compliance (Reference)

If anyone asks deeper questions, here are the four key concerns and your position:

1. ROS Terms of Service

Revenue Online Service (ROS) does not currently authorise automated/bot-driven form submissions. Any production integration would require explicit approval from Revenue or use of their official API (when available).

Our position: The demo shows the capability. We would only go live with Revenue's blessing or through an approved integration path.

2. Credential Handling

ROS authentication requires a digital certificate (.p12 file). Giving an AI agent access to this cert is a significant security decision — it's equivalent to handing over full access to file returns, view liabilities, and make payments.

Our position: We'd never store or transmit certs. Any integration would use a sandboxed browser session with the user's own authenticated session, or a future OAuth-style delegation from Revenue.

3. Liability

If an AI agent enters an incorrect figure on a tax return, liability is unclear. Is it the software provider? The accountant who approved it? The business owner?

Our position: Balnce always stops before submission. A human reviews and approves. The AI prepares — the human files. This keeps the liability chain clear.

4. GDPR / Data Protection

Tax data (income, expenses, PPS numbers, company details) is sensitive personal data under GDPR. Routing it through an AI agent (especially a cloud-hosted LLM) adds a data processor.

Our position: Balnce processes data in Supabase (EU-hosted). The OpenClaw agent runs locally on the user's machine — no tax data leaves their device.

6 Likely Questions & Answers

"How do you handle different business types?"

We have trade profiles built in — construction (RCT, two-thirds rule), SaaS (reverse charge VAT, R&D credits), general trades. The categorisation engine adapts based on the business type selected during onboarding.

"How accurate is the auto-categorisation?"

We use keyword matching against Irish VAT and CT rules, vendor name recognition, and receipt OCR. The user can review and override any categorisation. Uncategorised items are flagged as add-backs until reviewed.

"Who is this for?"

Irish limited companies with one or two directors. Primarily trades (construction, electrical, plumbing) and small tech/SaaS companies. These businesses typically pay €2,000-5,000/year for a basic accountant to do exactly what Balnce does automatically.

"What's the business model?"

SaaS subscription. The accountant is a €3,000/year cost that most small companies accept as unavoidable. We make it avoidable.

"Are you replacing accountants?"

We're replacing the manual data entry and compliance grunt work. Complex tax planning, audits, and advisory still need a human. But 80% of what a small-company accountant does is bookkeeping and filing — and that's what we automate.
